Water levels, notifications, and paddling information at your fingertips. Rivers.run includes information on over 10,000 rivers and creeks across the US, Canada, and UK/Ireland.

Rivers.run is your guide to whitewater (and flatwater) paddling, matching real-time gauge data with crowdsourced expertise. With data sourced from USGS, Environment Canada, and UK/Ireland gauges, rivers.run takes the guesswork out of river levels with flow notifications, color coding, and easy to use search & sort tools.

KEY FEATURES

Interactive Map & Weather
Discover local rivers, view live weather radar (US), and tap any run to see live gauge information, access points, and rapid descriptions.

Works Offline
Crucial for remote put-ins and deep canyons, rivers.run is designed to work offline. Cache your favorites, maps, and flow graphs before you leave — the app stays fully functional in dead zones.

Advanced Search & Filtering
Find your next trip by filtering rivers by current water levels, difficulty class, location, distance, custom tags, and more.

Custom Lists & Favorites
Build and organize your own river lists by region, difficulty, or trip plan.

Flow Notifications
Set alerts for your favorite runs and get notified when flows hit your preferred range.

Community-Driven
Can't find your favorite local creek? Don't agree with a runnable level? Our database is crowdsourced, allowing you to easily contribute and improve the map for fellow paddlers.

Rivers.run is free, open-source, and built by paddlers for paddlers.

River flows are subject to change and conditions can vary rapidly. Always exercise caution, wear appropriate safety gear, and verify local conditions before paddling.
